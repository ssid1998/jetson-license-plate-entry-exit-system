See the \href{pytorch-cat-dog.md\#training-metrics}{\texttt{Training Metrics}} from the previous page for a description of the statistics from the output above.\hypertarget{md_jetson-inference_2docs_2pytorch-plants_autotoc_md371}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Model Accuracy}{Model Accuracy}}\label{md_jetson-inference_2docs_2pytorch-plants_autotoc_md371}
On the Plant\+CLEF dataset of 10,475 images, training Res\+Net-\/18 takes approximately \texorpdfstring{$\sim$}{\string~}15 minutes per epoch on Jetson Nano, or around 8 hours to train the model for 35 epochs. Below is a graph for analyzing the training progression of epochs versus model accuracy:\+



At around epoch 30, the Res\+Net-\/18 model reaches 75\% Top-\/5 accuracy, and at epoch 65 it converges on 85\% Top-\/5 accuracy. Interestingly these points of stability and convergence for the model occur at similiar times for Res\+Net-\/18 that they did for the previous Cat/\+\+Dog model. \doxylink{requirements_The}{The} model\textquotesingle{}s Top-\/1 accuracy is 55\%, which we\textquotesingle{}ll find to be quite effective in practice, given the diversity and challenging content from the Plant\+CLEF dataset (i.\+e. multiple overlapping varieties of plants per image and many pictures of leaves and tree trunks that are virtually indistinguishable from one another). ~\newline


By default the training script is set to run for 35 epochs, but if you don\textquotesingle{}t wish to wait that long to test out your model, you can exit training early and proceed to the next step (optionally re-\/starting the training again later from where you left off). You can also download this completed model that was trained for a full 100 epochs from here:\+

Note that the models are saved under {\ttfamily jetson-\/inference/\+python/\+training/\+classification/\+data/\+plants/\+}, including a checkpoint from the latest epoch and the best-\/performing model that has the highest classification accuracy.
