\chapter{Introduction}
\label{ch:introduction}

\begin{center}
	\textbf{Project: Number Plate Detection using Jetson Nano}\\
	Edge deployment of YOLOv8-based real-time License Plate Recognition (LPR) and billing system for airport automation.
\end{center}

\vspace{0.4cm}

\section{Introduction}
Efficient and automated vehicle identification has become essential in smart transportation, particularly for airport parking and security control systems. Traditional Automated Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) solutions depend on high-cost proprietary cameras and centralized servers, limiting scalability and flexibility \cite{tang2022automatic}. The recent advancement of edge AI has enabled deployment of compact deep-learning models such as YOLOv8 directly on embedded hardware, enabling local, real-time decision-making without reliance on cloud processing \cite{jocher2023ultralytics, nvidia_jetson_whitepaper2021}.

This project focuses on designing a cost-effective, GDPR-compliant, single-camera LPR system optimized for the NVIDIA Jetson Nano. By integrating YOLOv8 for detection, EasyOCR for text extraction, and SQLite for data logging, the system enables entry/exit recognition and dwell-time-based billing. Its design demonstrates the convergence of artificial intelligence, embedded computing, and privacy-aware data management within modern transportation infrastructure \cite{mufti2021automatic, plavac2024performance}.

\vspace{0.2cm}

\section{Problem Description}
Airports face continuous operational challenges in managing thousands of vehicles daily. Manual verification is error-prone, time-consuming, and inconsistent, while commercial ANPR cameras are expensive and inflexible \cite{netinant2024evaluating}. German airports—such as Frankfurt, Munich, and Berlin—require rapid, accurate, and legally compliant vehicle identification systems that can operate under varying light, weather, and plate types \cite{fraportALPR, munichALPR}.

Key issues include:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{High Throughput:} Thousands of entries per day demand sub-second recognition to prevent congestion.
	\item \textbf{Diverse Plate Formats:} Germany’s 15+ plate variants (electric, diplomatic, export, etc.) pose recognition challenges \cite{mufti2021automatic}.
	\item \textbf{Environmental Variations:} Lighting changes, glare, and weather affect detection reliability \cite{plavac2024performance}.
	\item \textbf{Data Privacy:} Strict GDPR/BDSG regulations limit image retention and require on-site anonymization \cite{gdpr2016eu, bdsga2021}.
\end{itemize}

These constraints necessitate a locally deployed, energy-efficient, and privacy-preserving AI system capable of performing all detection and billing logic directly on edge hardware.

\vspace{0.2cm}

\section{Challenges }
Despite technological progress, several challenges persist in real-world ANPR deployment:

\subsection*{1. Environmental and Lighting Variability}
Rain, glare, and shadows often degrade OCR performance. Studies show that image preprocessing, adaptive exposure, and IR-assisted lighting can improve accuracy under adverse conditions \cite{sultan2023applied, tavarez2024comparison}.

\subsection*{2. Computational Limitations on Edge Devices}
Jetson Nano’s limited GPU capacity constrains real-time deep learning performance. Research emphasizes quantization, pruning, and TensorRT optimization to maintain 10–15 FPS without loss of accuracy \cite{laroca2018robust, nvidia_jetson_whitepaper2021}.

\subsection*{3. False Positives and Non-Standard Plates}
Objects resembling plates or partially visible characters can trigger incorrect detections. Multi-frame validation and spatial consistency checks with vehicle bounding boxes have proven effective against such errors \cite{mufti2021automatic, netinant2024evaluating}.

\subsection*{4. Privacy and Legal Compliance}
Since vehicle registration numbers are considered personal identifiers, storage must comply with GDPR principles of minimization and lawful processing \cite{gdpr2016eu, bdsga2021}. On-site anonymization and short retention periods are mandatory for lawful ANPR operation in Germany.

\subsection*{5. Cost and Scalability}
Commercial systems can cost €3,000–€8,000 per lane \cite{Jenoptik2023}. Open-source YOLO-based models on affordable edge devices enable democratized deployment across airports, campuses, and municipalities \cite{Ultralytics2023, JetsonNano2022}.

\vspace{0.2cm}

\section{Proposed Solution}
The proposed system introduces a lightweight, hybrid deep-learning architecture for real-time number plate recognition and billing at the edge.  
It combines:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{YOLOv8-Nano:} Continuous, low-latency detection of plates in live video streams.
	\item \textbf{YOLOv8-Large:} Selective re-validation of uncertain detections to boost accuracy under complex conditions \cite{jocher2023ultralytics, wang2023yolov8}.
	\item \textbf{EasyOCR:} Text extraction from cropped plate images with character-level confidence tracking.
	\item \textbf{SQLite Database:} Offline storage of entry/exit logs, timestamps, and dwell-time for automated billing.
\end{itemize}

Each module is optimized for energy-efficient edge inference using TensorRT and FP16 precision. The system automatically exempts special vehicle types—such as police, military, and diplomatic plates—by classifying color or prefix, ensuring compliance and fairness. 

To maintain privacy, all plate numbers are hashed before database storage, and images are automatically purged within a set retention period. The architecture supports scalability by connecting multiple Jetson Nano nodes for multi-lane deployment while maintaining local independence.
