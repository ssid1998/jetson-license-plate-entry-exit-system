\chapter{Introduction}

\begin{center}
	\textbf{Project: Number Plate Detection using Jetson Nano}\\
	Edge deployment of \textbf{YOLOv8}-based real-time \ac{LPR} and billing system for airport automation.
\end{center}

\vspace{0.4cm}

\section{Introduction}
Efficient and automated vehicle identification has become essential in smart transportation, particularly for airport parking and security control systems. Traditional \ac{ANPR} solutions depend on high-cost proprietary cameras and centralized servers, limiting scalability and flexibility \cite{tang2022automatic}. The recent advancement of edge AI has enabled deployment of compact deep-learning models such as \textbf{YOLOv8} directly on embedded hardware, enabling local, real-time decision-making without reliance on cloud processing \cite{jocher2023ultralytics, nvidia_jetson_whitepaper2021}.

This project focuses on designing a cost-effective, \ac{GDPR}-compliant, single-camera \ac{LPR} optimized for the \textbf{NVIDIA Jetson Nano}. By integrating \textbf{YOLOv8} for detection, \textbf{EasyOCR} for text extraction, and SQLite for data logging, the system enables entry/exit recognition and dwell-time-based billing. Its design demonstrates the convergence of \ac{AI}, embedded computing, and privacy-aware data management within modern transportation infrastructure \cite{mufti2021automatic, plavac2024performance}.

\vspace{0.2cm}

\section{Problem Description}
Airports face continuous operational challenges in managing thousands of vehicles daily. Manual verification is error-prone, time-consuming, and inconsistent, while commercial \ac{ANPR} cameras are expensive and inflexible \cite{netinant2024evaluating}. German airports—such as Frankfurt, Munich, and Berlin—require rapid, accurate, and legally compliant vehicle identification systems that can operate under varying light, weather, and plate types \cite{fraportALPR, munichALPR}.

Key issues include:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{High Throughput:} Thousands of entries per day demand sub-second recognition to prevent congestion.
	\item \textbf{Diverse Plate Formats:} Germany’s 15+ plate variants (electric, diplomatic, export, etc.) pose recognition challenges \cite{mufti2021automatic}.
	\item \textbf{Environmental Variations:} Lighting changes, glare, and weather affect detection reliability \cite{plavac2024performance}.
	\item \textbf{Data Privacy:} Strict \ac{GDPR}/\ac{BDSG} regulations limit image retention and require on-site anonymization \cite{gdpr2016eu, bdsga2021}.
\end{itemize}

These constraints necessitate a locally deployed, energy-efficient, and privacy-preserving \ac{AI} system capable of performing all detection and billing logic directly on edge hardware.

\section{Challenges}
Although \ac{ANPR} systems have advanced rapidly, several persistent obstacles still hinder efficient deployment on low-power edge devices. These challenges are particularly evident in airport automation environments, where performance, compliance, and reliability must coexist under real-world constraints.

\subsection*{1. Environmental and Lighting Variability}
Outdoor environments cause fluctuations in illumination due to sunlight, reflections, fog, and rain, which significantly affect the reliability of detection and text recognition.  
Studies such as \cite{sultan2023applied, tavarez2024comparison} have shown that varying brightness and weather conditions increase \ac{OCR} error rates and reduce \ac{MAP} in detection models. For airports operating 24/7, low-visibility conditions further degrade confidence in plate extraction.

\subsection*{2. Computational Limitations on Edge Devices}
Edge devices like the \textbf{NVIDIA Jetson Nano} have limited \ac{GPU} resources and memory compared to traditional servers.  
As noted by \cite{laroca2018robust, nvidia_jetson_whitepaper2021}, high-performance deep-learning models such as \textbf{YOLOv8} demand significant compute power, which can reduce frame rates to below real-time levels. Prolonged \ac{GPU} utilization also raises issues of overheating and throttling under continuous workloads.

\subsection*{3. False Positives and Non-Standard Plates}
Incorrect detections occur when objects with plate-like textures (advertisements, reflections, or vehicle logos) are misidentified as license plates.  
Research by \cite{mufti2021automatic, netinant2024evaluating} confirms that \ac{ANPR} accuracy deteriorates when encountering partially visible, dirty, or non-standard plates with decorative fonts or region-specific layouts.  
These false positives increase storage load and reduce system credibility in enforcement scenarios.

\subsection*{4. Privacy and Legal Compliance}
European regulations such as \ac{GDPR} and the German \ac{BDSG} require that personal identifiers, including license plate data, be processed under strict privacy principles.  
Legal frameworks emphasize data minimization, lawful processing, and transparency \cite{gdpr2016eu, bdsga2021}.  
Systems that store full-resolution images without anonymization may violate Article 5(1)(c) of \ac{GDPR}, posing compliance risks for public infrastructure deployments.

\subsection*{5. Cost and Scalability}
Commercial \ac{ANPR} systems—such as those from Jenoptik or PeterPark—are expensive, closed-source, and require recurring maintenance fees \cite{Jenoptik2023, peterparkALPR}.  
Research comparing \textbf{YOLO}-based implementations on low-cost edge hardware \cite{Ultralytics2023, JetsonNano2022} highlights the feasibility of democratizing intelligent surveillance through modular, open, and affordable architectures.  
However, scalability still demands efficient synchronization between multiple camera nodes and lightweight local databases.

\section{Proposed Solutions}
To address the above challenges, the project integrates a combination of hardware optimization, lightweight \ac{AI} design, and legal compliance mechanisms.  
Each proposed method directly corresponds to one identified issue, ensuring both technical robustness and regulatory alignment.

\subsection*{1. Addressing Environmental and Lighting Variability}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Apply dynamic image preprocessing using \textbf{OpenCV} to normalize brightness, gamma, and contrast across frames.
	\item Integrate IR-assisted illumination and adaptive exposure for stable night-time operation.
	\item Use deraining and dehazing filters to reduce weather-induced distortions before \textbf{YOLO} inference.
	\item Expand dataset diversity with day, night, and adverse-weather conditions to improve model generalization.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{2. Optimizing Computational Performance}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Quantize \textbf{YOLOv8} weights to \textbf{FP16} and optimize inference through TensorRT to achieve real-time speed.
	\item Use a hybrid configuration—\textbf{YOLOv8-Nano} for continuous inference and \textbf{YOLOv8-Large} for periodic re-validation of uncertain detections.
	\item Implement multi-threading and asynchronous pipelines to parallelize camera feed capture, inference, and \ac{OCR}.
	\item Monitor \ac{GPU} utilization via \textbf{Jetson-Stats} for automatic frame-rate adjustments under thermal constraints.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{3. Reducing False Positives and Handling Non-Standard Plates}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Introduce temporal validation across consecutive frames to confirm detection stability.
	\item Apply bounding-box correlation between vehicle and plate regions to prevent spurious matches.
	\item Enforce a 90\% minimum \ac{OCR} confidence threshold; reprocess ambiguous characters using EasyOCR fallback.
	\item Include a classification module for special plates (e.g., emergency, diplomatic) based on prefix and color cues.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{4. Ensuring Privacy and Legal Compliance}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Hash recognized plate numbers using \textbf{SHA-256} before database storage to ensure irreversible anonymization.
	\item Retain images temporarily ($\leq$24 hours) with automated deletion through a background cleanup scheduler.
	\item Encrypt all local \textbf{SQLite} databases and restrict external network communication.
	\item Display privacy notices on-site and document data-handling practices to comply with \ac{GDPR} transparency obligations.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{5. Improving Cost Efficiency and Scalability}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Use open-source tools (\textbf{YOLOv8}, \textbf{EasyOCR}, \textbf{SQLite}) to eliminate proprietary license costs.
	\item Develop a modular architecture separating hardware (camera), \ac{AI} inference, and database layers for flexible scaling.
	\item Implement \textbf{RESTful API} endpoints to synchronize logs between multiple Jetson Nano units in multi-lane environments.
	\item Package the setup into an installable deployment kit for plug-and-play installation across various airport terminals.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
In summary, this solution framework transforms a conventional centralized \ac{ANPR} system into a distributed, low-cost, and privacy-compliant edge network.  
By systematically addressing environmental, computational, and regulatory constraints, the proposed approach ensures scalability, sustainability, and long-term adaptability to evolving smart mobility infrastructures.
