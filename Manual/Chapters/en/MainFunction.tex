\section{Main Function}

The \textbf{Airport License Plate Detection System} is a smart camera-based solution that automatically recognises and records the license plates of vehicles entering and leaving the airport area.  
It is specially designed for \textbf{German license plates} and helps manage short-term parking at the airport gate efficiently and fairly.  

\vspace{0.5em}
When a vehicle enters, the camera captures the license plate and stores it together with the date and time.  
When the same vehicle exits, the main unit  detects it again and automatically calculates how long it stayed inside the airport area.  
If the duration is more than \textbf{15 minutes}, the main unit marks it for billing.  
If the stay is \textbf{15 minutes or less}, the parking is free of charge 

\vspace{0.5em}
Certain vehicles such as \textbf{emergency services (ambulance, police, fire brigade)} and \textbf{official government vehicles} are automatically identified and exempted from all charges.  
These special plates are pre-registered in the main unit’s database, ensuring that authorised vehicles always pass freely without manual intervention.

\vspace{0.5em}
All vehicle data, including plate number, entry time, exit time, duration, and payment status, are securely stored inside the device.  
This ensures transparency, traceability, and smooth traffic flow at both the entry and exit points of the airport

\noindent\textbf{Key Highlights:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Recognises and records German license plates automatically.
	\item Calculates parking duration and applies the 15-minute free rule.
	\item Automatically exempts emergency and government vehicles from charges.
	\item Works fully offline — no internet or network setup required.
	\item Stores all records locally for reporting, auditing, or payment.
\end{itemize}

\section{Billing Mechanism}

	\label{fig:sidebar_log}

The billing process automatically determines whether a vehicle should be charged based on its total parking duration. The workflow is divided into four main scenarios as described below.

\subsection{Scenario 1: Vehicle Entry}
\begin{itemize}
	\item When a car enters the premises, the system automatically detects and records the license plate number.
	\item The entry time is stored in the database with a timestamp.
	\item No payment is initiated at this stage.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scenario 2: Vehicle Exit}
\begin{itemize}
	\item On exit, the system again detects the license plate and retrieves the corresponding entry record.
	\item The current time is compared with the stored entry timestamp.
	\item The total parking duration is computed in minutes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scenario 3: Duration Less Than 15 Minutes}
\begin{itemize}
	\item If the total parking time is \textbf{less than 15 minutes}, the system classifies the session as \textbf{free of cost}.
	\item The display automatically redirects to the \textbf{“Danke” (Thank You)} page.
	\item The barrier opens and the vehicle exits without any payment.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scenario 4: Duration Greater Than 15 Minutes}
\begin{itemize}
	\item If the parking duration \textbf{exceeds 15 minutes}, the system calculates the billing amount at the rate of \textbf{€2.00 per hour (pro-rated)}.
	\item The total payable amount is shown on the display screen.
	\item The user completes the payment through the integrated terminal.
	\item Once the transaction is successful, the barrier opens and the system resets for the next vehicle.
\end{itemize}

\begin{center}
	\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
		\hline
		\textbf{Parking Duration} & \textbf{Billing Amount} \\ \hline
		0 -- 15 minutes & Free of Cost \\ \hline
		$>$ 15 minutes & €2.00 per hour (pro-rated) \\ \hline
	\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{flushleft}
	\textit{Example:}  
	If a car remains parked for 42 minutes, the payable amount is calculated as:
	\[
	\text{Amount} = \frac{42}{60} \times 2.00 = €1.40
	\]
	The user is then prompted to make the payment before exit authorization.
\end{flushleft}

\section{System Power Control}
To ensure the longevity of the Jetson Nano hardware and prevent corruption of the local SQLite database, the system must be shut down correctly at the end of the operating hours. 

\begin{figure}[H]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.4\textwidth]{general/shutDownbutton.png}
	\caption{The Software Shutdown/Stop Button located on the Dashboard.}
	\label{fig:stop_button}
\end{figure}

\noindent\textbf{Safe Shutdown Procedure:}
\begin{enumerate}
	\item Locate the red \textbf{Stop/Shutdown} button at the bottom of the dashboard sidebar.
	\item Click the button once; a confirmation dialogue will appear on the screen.
	\item Upon confirmation, the system will finalize all database writes and safely power off the Jetson Nano.
	\item Only after the screen goes completely dark should the physical power cable be disconnected.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Operational Environment and Setup}
\noindent\textbf{Camera Placement and Setup:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Mount the camera about \textbf{2.0–2.5 metres above ground level}, facing the vehicle lane at a \textbf{30–40° angle}.
	\item Keep a distance of about \textbf{3–5 metres} between the camera and the point where vehicles stop or slow down.
	\item Ensure there is enough ambient light. During night hours, add a small \textbf{LED lamp or floodlight} near the gate for better clarity.
	\item Keep the camera lens clean and free of dust, water spots, or scratches.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Plausibility and Manual Verification:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item If the main unit cannot read a plate clearly (for example, due to dirt, glare, or motion blur), it will display a \textbf{“Detection Unclear”} message on the screen.
	\item In such cases, the operator can manually enter the license plate number using a simple on-screen input field.
	\item The main unit checks for \textbf{duplicate or incomplete entries} to avoid double billing.
	\item If an entry or exit is missing, the software automatically flags the record for review.
\end{itemize}

\section{User Interaction}
The system is designed for daily airport use with minimal user effort. All detections, timings, and charge decisions appear automatically on the display.

\vspace{0.6em}
\noindent\textbf{Interface Overview:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item The screen displays the \textbf{camera view} on the left and the \textbf{detection log} on the right.
	\item Each entry shows the plate number, time, duration, and parking status — “Free” (green) or “Charge” (orange).
	\item Exempt vehicles such as police or ambulance appear in blue with “No Charge.”
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.6em}
\noindent\textbf{Daily Operation:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Power on — the system starts automatically and the AI engine initializes.
	\item Observe the live feed and alerts for any unreadable plates.
	\item Use the \textbf{Stop Button} (Fig. \ref{fig:stop_button}) to turn off the display and power after working hours; all data is saved automatically.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4cm}
\noindent
This workflow ensures clear, automatic, and reliable operation for non-technical staff, focusing on smooth vehicle flow and accurate billing.
