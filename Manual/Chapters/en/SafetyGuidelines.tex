\chapter{Safety Guidelines}

\section{Safety Guidelines}

The following safety guidelines are essential to ensure the reliable, secure, and fault-free operation of the \textbf{License Plate Detection System}.  
They cover electrical, thermal, mechanical, and operational aspects relevant to the embedded AI hardware setupx.

\vspace{0.4cm}
\subsection{Electrical Safety}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Always use a certified \textbf{5V / 4A USB-C power supply} with built-in safety protection against overcurrent and short-circuits.  
	\item Avoid connecting or disconnecting peripherals (camera, HDMI, USB) while the main unit is powered on.  
	\item Check all cables for damage or wear before use, and make sure they are properly insulated.  
	\item Use grounded power outlets or a surge protector to prevent voltage spikes or static discharge.  
	\item Connecting cables incorrectly may cause permanent damage to the board or its peripherals.  
	\item Avoid operating the main unit outdoors during rain, lightning, or unstable weather conditions.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4cm}
\subsection{Thermal and Mechanical Safety}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Ensure good ventilation around the aluminum case — do not block any openings, as the main unit relies on passive cooling.  
	\item Keep the device within a safe temperature range (\textbf{0\,\textdegree C to 40\,\textdegree C}).  
	In hotter environments, use a small fan to maintain airflow.  
	\item Keep liquids, food, or heavy objects away from the unit. Avoid high humidity environments.  
	\item Mount the camera securely and ensure it cannot fall or shift during operation.  
	\item When handling the main unit's  board directly, use anti-static precautions (e.g., an ESD wrist strap).
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4cm}
\subsection{Operational Safety}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Always shut down the main unit properly using software commands (e.g., \texttt{sudo shutdown}) before unplugging power.  
	\item Do not overload the USB ports with high-power accessories or unverified devices.  
	\item Monitor for abnormal heating, flickering, or warning messages — if detected, power off immediately and inspect hardware.  
	\item Avoid sudden reboots or forced shutdowns while detections or database operations are running.  
	\item During cleaning or relocation, ensure the power supply is fully disconnected.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4cm}
\subsection{Power and Inference Safety}

Continuous AI processing generates both heat and power load.  
To keep the main unit safe and stable:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Periodically check the power draw using a USB power meter or multimeter — it should remain within the adapter’s rated limit.  
	\item Avoid keeping the main unit under heavy load for long hours without monitoring temperature.  
	\item Allow short cooling breaks after extended operation or testing sessions.  
\end{itemize}
