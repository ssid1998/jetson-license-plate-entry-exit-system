This section explains, in simple steps, how to prepare and start the \textbf {License Plate Detection System}. Follow the setup steps below to get the product ready for usage:

\vspace{0.4cm}
\noindent
\textbf{Step 1: Place and Connect the Hardware}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Place the \textbf{main unit} on a stable, well-ventilated surface.
	\item Mount the \textbf{camera} on the camera stand and point it toward the vehicle lane or entry gate.
	\item Connect the camera to the main unit using the USB Type A cable pre-built with the camera.
	\item Connect the \textbf{HDMI cable} from the main unit to the monitor.
	\item Insert the \textbf{microSD card} into the main unit.
	\item Connect the \textbf{microSD card} into the main unit.
	\item Connect the \textbf{mouse} to the main unit through its USB type A port.	
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent
\textbf{Step 2: Start the Main Unit}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Plug in the \textbf{power cord} for the display and turn the display on using its power on button.  	
	\item Plug in the \textbf{power adapter} and connect it with the main unit using it's C-type DC-IN outlet.\textbf {Important:} Connect this power supply strictly with the DC-IN port at the left position on the back side, not the USB-C port at the right position on the back side.
	\item Wait for a few seconds for the main unit to start.  
	\item A green LED indicator will turn on, indicating successful boot. If it isn't glowing, it indicates a problem with the main unit. 

\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent
\textbf{Step 3: Verify Operation}
\begin{itemize}
	\item After detection, any detected car license plates are logged with timestamps and saved. Additionally, the display will show the recognized license plate with entry time and subsequently, exit time at the time of exit.  
	\item Adjust the camera height or tilt if the plates are not clearly visible.
\item Ensure good lighting at the vehicle gate area.
For accurate plate reading, maintain an illumination level between 
\textbf{250 lux (minimum)} and \textbf{900 lux (maximum)} — with an 
optimal range of \textbf{400–700 lux}. 
Avoid shining lights directly into the camera to prevent glare or overexposure.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent
\textbf{Step 4: During and After Use}
\begin{itemize}
	\item The product runs continuously and does not need supervision.  
	\item After use, if the product is to be shut down, the shut down button in the display interface can be clicked using the mouse to safely shut the product off.   
	\item All data is stored safely for billing and reporting.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent

\vspace{0.8em}
\noindent
\section{Camera Placement and Setup:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Mount the camera about \textbf{2.0–2.5 metres above ground level}, facing the vehicle lane at a \textbf{30–40° angle}.
	\item Keep a distance of about \textbf{3–5 metres} between the camera and the point where vehicles stop or slow down.
	\item Avoid placing the camera directly opposite bright lights or reflective surfaces.
	\item Ensure there is enough ambient light. During night hours, add a small \textbf{LED lamp or floodlight} near the gate for better clarity.
	\item Keep the camera lens clean and free of dust, water spots, or scratches.
	\item Do not position the camera behind glass or near strong sunlight to prevent reflections.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.8em}

\section*{Important Tips}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Place the device on a stable, flat surface with good ventilation.
	\item Make sure the \textbf{power supply (5 V / 4 A)} is connected firmly and the cable is not loose.
	\item Keep the \textbf{camera lens clean} for accurate plate reading.
	\item Do not block the openings on the aluminum case; it keeps the main unit cool.
	\item Avoid exposing the device to rain, moisture, or direct sunlight.
	\item Connect the HDMI cable to your monitor before switching on the power.
	\item Wait until the indicator light turns on — this means the entire sytem is ready.
	\item If the display stays blank, check power and camera connections first.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent
These simple steps help ensure your product starts safely and performs reliably every time.
